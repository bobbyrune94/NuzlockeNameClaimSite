How to Call Commands

To see all available commands that the bot has, type forward slash (/) and a menu should pop up with all available commands for all the discord bots in the server. Look for the seedot icon (the icon for this bot) and click that. That will bring up all the available commands registered in this bot.

On mobile devices, it’ll look like this:

To choose a command, either click on it from the selection or start typing the command name and discord will try to autocomplete it for you. You can use your up/down arrow keys to move your selection and hit ENTER or click on the one you intend to use.

When you select a command, you’ll likely see something like the image below. The boxes indicate arguments that you need to pass to the command.

  1. Input a name for the pokemon you wish to claim,
  2. You can either click into the box labeled “male-nickname” or press TAB. Mobile users can tap that box. Input your nickname for a pokemon of that species that is male.
  3. Click into the “female-nickname” box or press TAB again. Once again, mobile users can tap that box. Input your nickname for a pokemon of that species that is female.
  4. You may click back into any of the boxes again to fix any typos or misspellings. Once you are satisfied, press ENTER and let the bot do its magic.

Commands

/Directions

Gives an overview of all of the commands you can use with the discord bot.

/Claim

Claims a Pokemon and its evolutionary line for the streamer to use in their playthrough. Each claim requires you to input a Pokemon name and the nickname that you want the streamer to use for that Pokemon.

There are two subcommands associated with this:

  • Default: gives a nickname to the Pokemon no matter the gender. Ex: /claim default eevee fluffy

  • Gendered: allows you to give a nickname to a Pokemon depending on if it’s a male or female. When you input the nicknames, the male nickname comes first. Ex: /claim gendered eevee adam eve

/View

View the claim data for either your own claim or for a given Pokemon.

There are two subcommands associated with this:

  • Claim: Views the claim that you made in the system. Ex: /view claim

  • Pokemon: Views the claim data for the Pokemon that you choose. Ex: /view pokemon eevee

/Edit

Edits the nickname for all of your claim(s).

There are two subcommands associated with this:

  • Default: changes the nickname to be one not attached to any gender. Ex: /edit default fluffy

  • Gendered: changes the nickname to be one dependent on whether the caught Pokemon is male or female. The male nickname comes first when you input the two nicknames. Ex: /edit gendered king queen

/Change

Changes your claim from one Pokemon evolutionary line to another. You can only do this once every 3 months.

Like /claim and /edit, there are two subcommands associated with this:

  • Default: the new nickname will not be associated with the Pokemon’s gender. Ex: /change default shinx tiger

  • Gendered: the new nickname will depend on the gender of the caught Pokemon. The male nickname comes first. Ex: /change gendered shinx tiger tigress

/Remove

Removes your claim from the streamer’s claim table and allows anyone else to claim the Pokemon evolutionary line you gave up. However, you cannot make another claim until 3 months after you initially made that claim. This was done so that people who wanted to change their claims early couldn’t simply remove, then claim again.
